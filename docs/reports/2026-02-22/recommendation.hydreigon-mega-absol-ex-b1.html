Game Plan

Hydreigon Mega Absol ex is a control-oriented Dark deck that leverages poison damage, chip damage, and disruption to grind out wins. The core strategy revolves around using Rare Candy to accelerate Hydreigon into play, then using its ability to power up Mega Absol ex for efficient knockouts. Poison Barb and Nihilego apply consistent poison pressure, while Lucky Ice Pop provides healing to sustain your attackers. Cyrus disrupts opponent setup, and Copycat/Professor's Research maintain card advantage. The deck excels at controlling the pace of the game and punishing opponents who cannot quickly establish board presence.

Key Cards and Roles

Hydreigon

Stage 2 Dark attacker and energy accelerator. Central to the deck's strategy for powering up Mega Absol ex and applying pressure.

Mega Absol ex

Primary finisher. High-damage Dark attacker that benefits from Hydreigon's energy acceleration.

Rare Candy

Critical evolution accelerator. Allows Deino to skip Zweilous and evolve directly into Hydreigon, dramatically speeding up setup.

Poison Barb

Tool card that inflicts poison on the opponent's active Pokémon, providing consistent chip damage each turn.

Nihilego

Applies poison and spreads damage. Synergizes with Poison Barb to maximize poison pressure.

Lucky Ice Pop

Healing item that removes damage from your Pokémon, extending their longevity in prolonged games.

Cyrus

Disruption supporter that forces the opponent to shuffle cards back into their deck, slowing their setup.

Copycat

Draw supporter that matches opponent's hand size, providing flexible card advantage.

Game Plan Stages

Open

Copycat

Prioritize getting Deino into play on turn 1 using Poké Ball or natural draws. Use Professor's Research aggressively to dig for Deino, Rare Candy, and Hydreigon. If you open with Mega Absol ex or Nihilego, consider starting with them to apply early pressure or poison. Attach Poison Barb to your active Pokémon as soon as possible to begin poison damage accumulation. Avoid overcommitting resources early—focus on establishing the Deino-to-Hydreigon evolution line. Use Copycat to maintain hand size if you don't need to discard with Professor's Research. Bench positioning matters: keep Deino safe and prepare for Rare Candy evolution on turn 2 or 3.

Mid

Deino

Execute the Rare Candy play to evolve Deino directly into Hydreigon, bypassing Zweilous. Once Hydreigon is online, begin accelerating Dark energy to Mega Absol ex to enable its high-damage attacks. Use Nihilego to spread poison and chip damage across the opponent's board. Apply Lucky Ice Pop strategically to heal key attackers like Mega Absol ex or Hydreigon, keeping them in knockout range longer. Deploy Cyrus when the opponent has a strong hand to disrupt their momentum. Maintain poison pressure with Poison Barb and Nihilego—this passive damage adds up quickly. Use Sabrina (if included) to pull up vulnerable benched Pokémon for easier knockouts. Balance between applying offensive pressure and preserving your board state.

Close

Hydreigon

Leverage Mega Absol ex's high damage output to secure knockouts on opponent's primary threats. Calculate total damage including poison ticks to optimize knockout timing—don't overcommit energy if poison will finish the job. Use Lucky Ice Pop to keep Mega Absol ex or Hydreigon alive through critical turns. If the opponent is low on resources, use Cyrus to further disrupt their recovery. Nihilego can serve as a secondary attacker or poison spreader if Mega Absol ex is knocked out. Manage your energy attachments carefully—Hydreigon's acceleration is key to maintaining offensive pressure. Close out games by forcing the opponent into unfavorable trades where poison damage and chip damage accumulate faster than they can respond.

Tech Choices

  • Poison Barb (A3-146): Core tech for consistent poison application. Synergizes with Nihilego and the deck's attrition gameplan.
  • Nihilego (A3a-42): Provides poison spreading and additional damage output. Essential for maximizing poison synergy.
  • Lucky Ice Pop (B2-145): Healing tech that extends the lifespan of key attackers in grindy matchups.
  • Cyrus (A2-150): Disruption supporter that slows opponent setup and punishes large hands.
  • Sabrina (A1-225): Included in ~67% of lists for gust effects, enabling knockouts on benched threats or forcing unfavorable active Pokémon.
  • Absol (A4-120): Appears in ~33% of lists as a non-EX Dark attacker option, potentially for early game pressure or prize trade efficiency.
