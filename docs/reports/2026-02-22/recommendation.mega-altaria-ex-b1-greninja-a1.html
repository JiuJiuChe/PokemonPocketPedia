Game Plan

Mega Altaria ex Greninja is a dual-threat control deck that leverages Greninja's chip damage and Mega Altaria ex's tanky presence to grind out wins. The deck aims to establish board control early with Froakie/Greninja while using Rare Candy to accelerate Mega Altaria ex into play. Cyrus and Copycat provide hand disruption and resource management, while Chingling and Jirachi offer flexible utility. The deck excels at applying consistent pressure through Greninja's ability to damage multiple targets while Mega Altaria ex absorbs hits and closes games. With a 53.06% win rate and rising meta share (+1.74% over 7 days), this deck is a strong ladder choice for players who enjoy tactical, resource-efficient gameplay.

Key Cards and Roles

Greninja

Core damage engine. Applies chip damage across the opponent's board, softening targets for knockouts and enabling multi-prize turns. Essential for early-to-mid game pressure.

Mega Altaria ex

Primary attacker and tank. High HP and damage output make it the deck's closer. Use Rare Candy to accelerate evolution and bypass stage 1.

Rare Candy

Critical acceleration tool. Enables turn 2-3 Mega Altaria ex, bypassing Altaria and speeding up your win condition.

Cyrus

Disruption supporter. Forces opponent to discard resources, slowing their setup and creating tempo advantages.

Copycat

Flexible draw support. Provides hand refresh and can capitalize on opponent's large hand sizes for card advantage.

Froakie

Greninja line starter. Prioritize benching early to enable Greninja evolution by turn 2-3.

Swablu

Mega Altaria ex line starter. Bench alongside Froakie to set up both evolution lines.

Chingling

Utility bench sitter (67% presence). Provides additional disruption or stall options depending on matchup.

Game Plan Stages

Open

Froakie

Prioritize benching Froakie and Swablu on turn 1 to establish both evolution lines. Use Poké Ball and Professor's Research aggressively to find these basics and Rare Candy. Ideal opening hand includes Froakie, Swablu, and at least one draw supporter. Avoid overcommitting resources early—focus on setting up Greninja by turn 2-3 for chip damage while holding Rare Candy for Mega Altaria ex acceleration. If you open with Chingling or Jirachi, bench them only if you have spare bench space and expect utility value. Mulligan aggressively if you lack basics or draw support.

Mid

Greninja

Transition into active Greninja pressure by turn 2-3, using its ability to spread damage across the opponent's board. Simultaneously, use Rare Candy to evolve Swablu directly into Mega Altaria ex, bypassing Altaria. Deploy Cyrus to disrupt opponent's hand and slow their setup, especially against combo or evolution-heavy decks. Use Copycat to maintain hand size and dig for missing pieces. Position Mega Altaria ex on the bench until it's ready to take over as the primary attacker. Manage bench space carefully—prioritize Greninja, Mega Altaria ex, and one utility Pokémon (Chingling or Jirachi). Avoid overextending into vulnerable positions; Greninja's chip damage should soften targets for Mega Altaria ex to clean up efficiently.

Close

Mega Altaria ex

Shift to Mega Altaria ex as the primary attacker once Greninja has spread sufficient damage. Use Mega Altaria ex's high HP and damage output to take knockouts on softened targets, leveraging Greninja's prior chip damage for efficient prize trades. Cyrus remains critical in the late game to disrupt opponent's comeback attempts by forcing discards. Maintain board presence with Greninja on the bench to continue applying pressure if needed. Prioritize knocking out high-value targets (ex Pokémon or evolved threats) to maximize prize efficiency. If the opponent stabilizes, use Lisia to fetch disruption tools or additional Rare Candy for backup evolution lines. Avoid overcommitting Mega Altaria ex into unfavorable trades—retreat and reposition if necessary to preserve your win condition.
