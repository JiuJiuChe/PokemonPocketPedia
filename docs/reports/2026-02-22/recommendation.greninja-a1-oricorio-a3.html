Game Plan

Greninja Oricorio is a control-oriented deck that leverages Rare Candy to accelerate Greninja into play, using Oricorio and Pichu as support attackers. The deck aims to disrupt opponents with hand control Supporters (Cyrus, Ilima, Serena) while building board presence. Mega Kangaskhan ex provides additional offensive pressure. The strategy revolves around efficient evolution sequencing and resource denial to outlast opponents in the midgame.

Key Cards and Roles

Greninja

Primary attacker and win condition. Rare Candy enables turn 2-3 evolution to bypass Frogadier.

Oricorio

Support attacker providing early pressure and type coverage.

Pichu

Early-game attacker or bench utility to apply chip damage.

Rare Candy

Critical acceleration tool to evolve Froakie directly into Greninja, bypassing stage 1.

Cyrus

Hand disruption suite to limit opponent options and force suboptimal plays.

Irida

Water-type search support to find key Pokemon or Items.

Mega Kangaskhan ex

Secondary attacker for closing games or applying pressure.

Game Plan Stages

Open

Greninja

Prioritize benching Froakie and using Poké Ball or Irida to secure evolution pieces. Play Pichu or Oricorio as active to apply early pressure while setting up Greninja. Use Professor's Research aggressively to dig for Rare Candy and Froakie/Greninja. Avoid overextending bench space—reserve slots for Mega Kangaskhan ex if needed. Ideal turn 1-2 involves Froakie on bench, Rare Candy in hand, and a basic attacker active.

Mid

Oricorio

Deploy Greninja via Rare Candy as soon as possible (turn 2-3). Use Cyrus, Ilima, or Serena to disrupt opponent's hand and delay their setup. Oricorio and Pichu maintain board pressure while Greninja takes over as primary attacker. Manage energy attachments carefully to power Greninja's attacks. Use Copycat to refuel hand after disruption plays. Serena can force key bench targets active to stall or KO vulnerable Pokemon. Maintain tempo by denying opponent resources while building your own board state.

Close

Pichu

Greninja should be your primary closer, leveraging its evolved stats and attacks. Mega Kangaskhan ex can finish weakened targets or apply final pressure. Use remaining disruption Supporters to prevent opponent comebacks. Prioritize KOing threats that can OHKO Greninja. If Greninja is KO'd, pivot to Mega Kangaskhan ex or Oricorio to secure remaining prizes. Manage prize trade carefully—avoid unfavorable exchanges against high-HP ex Pokemon unless you can secure the final KO.

Tech Choices

  • Cyrus (A2-150): Hand disruption to limit opponent options, especially strong against combo decks.
  • Ilima (A3-149): Coin-flip based hand disruption; high-risk, high-reward against opponents with large hands.
  • Serena (B1a-69): Forces bench Pokemon active, enabling KOs on vulnerable targets or stalling evolving Pokemon.
  • Irida (A2a-72): Searches Water Pokemon or Items, improving consistency for finding Froakie, Greninja, or Rare Candy.
  • Mega Kangaskhan ex (B2-127): Provides secondary offensive threat and type diversity.
