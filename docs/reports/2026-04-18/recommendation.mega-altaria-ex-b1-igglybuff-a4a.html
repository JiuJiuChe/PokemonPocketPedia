Game Plan

This archetype is a balanced midrange shell built around a stable Mega Altaria ex line plus Darkrai/Igglybuff support package, with strong consistency and survivability trainers. Core identity is very rigid in samples: Darkrai, Igglybuff, Mega Altaria ex, Swablu, and Professor's Research are all avg_count 2.0 with presence_rate 1.0, while utility is spread across multiple 1-of supporters/items (Copycat, Cyrus, Giant Cape, Lisia, Pokémon Center Lady, Sabrina). Ranked plan is to establish Mega Altaria ex pressure consistently, then use Darkrai-side pressure and singleton interaction to maintain tempo through opposing pivots.

Game Plan Stages

Open

Darkrai

Prioritize opening access to at least one key basic (Swablu, Igglybuff, or Darkrai) plus a consistency enabler (Professor's Research or Poké Ball). In first turns, develop Mega Altaria line while keeping a second attacker route available so early disruption does not collapse your pressure. Use Professor's Research proactively when it improves evolution assembly; avoid firing singleton disruption supporters with no immediate board conversion.

Mid

Igglybuff

Once Mega Altaria ex is online, maintain pressure by sequencing attacks and pivots so Darkrai can cover awkward turns or resource gaps. Use Leaf and supporter one-ofs as tactical tempo tools rather than default plays, since each slot is high opportunity-cost in a compact list. Lean into durability loops (Giant Cape / Pokémon Center Lady) when board state suggests attrition, especially in midrange mirrors where preserving one active threat for an extra turn is high leverage.

Close

Mega Altaria ex

In closing turns, commit resources to the attacker line that gives the shortest finish route while preserving one interaction card for the opponent’s final stabilization turn. If Mega Altaria line is checked, transition to Darkrai-led pressure instead of rebuilding from zero. Prioritize guaranteed attack continuity and board retention over greedy hand sculpting once prize race is close.

Common Pitfalls

  • Overusing singleton supporters early and entering midgame without interaction options.
  • Ignoring board durability timing; Giant Cape and Pokémon Center Lady are strongest when used to force extra attack turns, not as default plays.
  • Overcommitting only to Mega Altaria line and failing to stage Darkrai as a backup pressure route.
  • Teching away from high-presence core cards despite their 100% presence_rate in sampled lists.
  • Treating category=None substitute suggestions as guaranteed upgrades; many are archetype pivots, not like-for-like replacements.
