Game Plan

Suicune ex Greninja ex is a Water-type control deck that leverages Suicune ex as a durable early-game attacker while building toward Greninja ex as a late-game finisher. The deck uses Rare Candy to accelerate Greninja evolution, bypassing Frogadier to quickly establish board presence. Cyrus and Mars provide hand disruption to slow opponents, while Irida searches Water Pokémon and Items. Indeedee ex offers utility support. The strategy revolves around controlling tempo with Suicune ex's bulk, disrupting opponent resources, and closing games with Greninja ex's high damage output.

Game Plan Stages

Open

Cyrus

Prioritize getting Froakie and Suicune ex into play early. Use Poké Ball and Professor's Research to search for Pokémon and draw into key cards. Ideally, start with Suicune ex active to apply early pressure while benching Froakie. Use Irida if available to search for Water Pokémon (Froakie or Suicune ex) and Items (Rare Candy or Giant Cape). Avoid overcommitting resources—focus on establishing board presence with 1-2 Froakie on bench and Suicune ex active. Hold Rare Candy for turn 2-3 to evolve Froakie directly into Greninja.

Mid

Froakie

Use Suicune ex to trade favorably while building energy on Greninja line. Deploy Rare Candy to evolve Froakie into Greninja (or Greninja ex if in hand), bypassing Frogadier entirely. Play Cyrus and Mars to disrupt opponent's hand and slow their setup. Attach Giant Cape to Suicune ex or Greninja ex to increase survivability. Use Irida to fetch missing evolution pieces or Items. Manage energy attachments carefully—prioritize Suicune ex for immediate pressure, but begin loading Greninja ex once it's on board. Indeedee ex can be benched for utility support. Maintain board control and deny opponent's key plays through disruption.

Close

Greninja

Transition from Suicune ex to Greninja ex as your primary attacker once fully powered. Greninja ex should deliver high-damage attacks to KO opponent's threats. Use Cyrus to strip opponent's hand before critical turns, limiting their response options. If Suicune ex is still healthy, keep it active to tank hits while Greninja ex charges. Greninja (non-ex) can serve as a backup attacker if Greninja ex is threatened. Time your attacks to secure KOs on high-value targets. Leverage HP advantage from Giant Cape to survive counterattacks. Close games by applying consistent pressure and denying opponent's comeback through hand disruption.

Tech Choices

  • Mars (1x): Additional hand disruption Supporter alongside Cyrus. Increases control density to disrupt opponent consistency.
  • Giant Cape (1x): HP-boosting Tool card. Enhances survivability of key attackers (Suicune ex or Greninja ex) to survive critical damage thresholds.
  • Indeedee ex (1x): Utility ex Pokémon. Exact role unclear from context, but likely provides support effects (draw, search, or board manipulation).
  • Irida (1x): Water-specific search Supporter. Fetches Water Pokémon and Items, improving consistency for evolution lines and key Items like Rare Candy.
