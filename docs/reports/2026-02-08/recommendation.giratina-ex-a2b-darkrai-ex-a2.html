Game Plan

Giratina ex Darkrai ex is a dual-threat Dark-type control deck that leverages Giratina ex's disruption and Darkrai ex's offensive pressure to grind out wins. The deck aims to establish board control through Rocky Helmet chip damage, Cyrus/Mars hand disruption, and strategic switching with Sabrina. With a 51.15% win rate and 4% meta share, this deck excels at outlasting opponents through attrition while maintaining consistent damage output. Indeedee ex provides early-game utility and energy acceleration, while Peculiar Plaza and Starting Plains offer stadium control. The deck's strength lies in its flexibility to pivot between aggressive Darkrai ex attacks and defensive Giratina ex stalling depending on matchup dynamics.

Key Cards and Roles

Giratina ex

Darkrai ex

Secondary offensive threat. Provides consistent Dark-type damage output and pressures opponents when Giratina ex is setting up or recovering.

Game Plan Stages

Open

Darkrai ex

Prioritize getting Indeedee ex into play early for energy acceleration and setup support. Use Professor's Research and Copycat to dig for Giratina ex or Darkrai ex while deploying Poké Ball to search for missing pieces. Attach Rocky Helmet to your anticipated active attacker (typically Giratina ex) to begin accumulating chip damage. Establish Starting Plains or Peculiar Plaza for stadium control. Avoid overextending—focus on building a stable board with both main attackers accessible. If facing aggressive decks, prioritize Giratina ex setup for defensive stalling; against slower decks, accelerate Darkrai ex for early pressure. Use Red sparingly to preserve resources for mid-game pivots.

Mid

Giratina ex

Transition into active disruption by deploying Cyrus and Mars to constrain opponent's hand size and limit their response options. Use Sabrina strategically to target damaged bench Pokémon or force unfavorable active matchups that favor your attackers. Maintain Rocky Helmet pressure on your active Pokémon to accumulate passive damage. Pivot between Giratina ex and Darkrai ex based on matchup needs—use Giratina ex to wall threatening attackers and Darkrai ex to apply consistent offensive pressure. Attach Giant Cape to your primary attacker to increase survivability. Use Lucky Ice Pop to heal critical damage and extend your attacker's lifespan. Control the stadium war with Peculiar Plaza to deny opponent benefits. Manage energy attachments carefully to keep both attackers threatening.

Close

Poké Ball

Secure KOs through accumulated Rocky Helmet damage combined with Darkrai ex's attacks to close out games efficiently. Use Sabrina to pull up damaged bench targets for easy prizes. Deploy Cyrus/Mars during critical turns to prevent opponent comebacks by limiting their hand resources. If ahead on prizes, switch to Giratina ex to stall and deny opponent opportunities to mount a comeback. Use Red to recycle key supporters or tools if needed for final push. Maintain board presence with both attackers to threaten multiple angles of attack. In close games, leverage Lucky Ice Pop healing to survive one additional turn and secure the final KO. Prioritize prize efficiency—target ex Pokémon only when guaranteed KOs are available, otherwise chip away at single-prize threats.

Tech Choices

  • Rocky Helmet (2x): Essential for the attrition gameplan, punishing every opponent attack and accelerating prize race through passive damage.
  • Cyrus + Mars (1x each): Hand disruption package that creates resource scarcity for opponents, particularly effective against combo and setup-heavy decks.
  • Indeedee ex (1x): Provides early-game acceleration and utility, enabling faster setup of main attackers.
  • Giant Cape (1x): Increases HP threshold for key attackers, making them survive additional hits in critical matchups.
  • Peculiar Plaza + Starting Plains (1x each): Stadium control package that denies opponent stadium benefits while potentially providing deck-specific utility.
  • Lucky Ice Pop (1x): Healing tech that extends game length in favorable attrition matchups.
  • Sabrina (1x): Gust effect for targeting bench Pokémon and forcing unfavorable trades.
  • Red (1x): Resource recovery supporter for late-game flexibility and recycling key cards.
