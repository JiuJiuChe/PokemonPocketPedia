Game Plan

Mega Altaria ex Chingling is a control-oriented deck that leverages Chingling's disruption alongside Mega Altaria ex's bulk and damage output. The strategy revolves around stalling with Chingling early, using Cyrus and Lisia for hand disruption and deck searching, then transitioning to Mega Altaria ex as your primary attacker. Rocky Helmet punishes opponents for attacking, while Tapu Lele and Jirachi provide utility. Training Area accelerates evolution setup. The deck aims to outlast opponents through disruption, chip damage, and a tanky Mega evolution finisher.

Game Plan Stages

Open

Chingling

Prioritize getting Chingling active turn 1 to disrupt opponent's setup. Use Professor's Research aggressively to dig for Swablu and evolution pieces. Bench Swablu immediately when found. Play Training Area early if drawn to accelerate your evolution timeline. Use Copycat or Professor's Research to maintain hand size while setting up. Attach Rocky Helmet to Chingling if available to punish early aggression. Avoid overextending—focus on establishing Chingling stall while building your Altaria line on the bench.

Mid

Cyrus

Transition from Chingling to Mega Altaria ex once evolution is ready. Use Cyrus strategically to disrupt opponent's hand when they're setting up key plays. Lisia becomes critical for finding missing evolution pieces or tools. Manage energy attachments to power up Mega Altaria ex while keeping backup attackers ready. Use Lucky Ice Pop to heal Mega Altaria ex if it takes early damage. Deploy Tapu Lele or Jirachi for utility as needed. Rocky Helmet should be on your active attacker to maximize chip damage. Control the pace—don't rush into unfavorable trades.

Close

Lisia

Mega Altaria ex becomes your primary win condition. Use its high HP and damage output to take knockouts while tanking hits. Cyrus disrupts opponent's comeback attempts by limiting their hand resources. Rocky Helmet chip damage accumulates to secure knockouts on weakened targets. Lucky Ice Pop extends Mega Altaria ex's longevity in critical moments. Red Card (if included) can disrupt opponent's final plays. Maintain board control—don't let opponent set up multiple threats. Close games by leveraging your HP advantage and consistent damage output while denying opponent resources.
