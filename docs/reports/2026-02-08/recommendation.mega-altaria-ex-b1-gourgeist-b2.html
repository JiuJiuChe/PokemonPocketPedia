Game Plan

Mega Altaria ex Gourgeist is a control-oriented deck that leverages Gourgeist's disruptive abilities alongside Mega Altaria ex's tanky presence and Chingling's support. The deck aims to slow down opponents with hand disruption (Cyrus, Mars), stall with Gourgeist's healing and damage, and close games with Mega Altaria ex's sustained damage output. Training Area accelerates evolution lines, while the supporter suite provides flexibility in draw and disruption. This deck excels in grindy matchups where resource denial and incremental advantage win games.

Key Cards and Roles

Mega Altaria ex

Primary win condition. Tanky ex attacker with high HP and consistent damage output. Requires Swablu evolution setup.

Gourgeist

Disruptive attacker with healing and damage capabilities. Evolves from Pumpkaboo. Key for stalling and controlling tempo.

Chingling

Support Pokémon providing utility (likely energy acceleration or draw support based on Psychic typing). Enables faster setup.

Cyrus

Hand disruption supporters. Cyrus forces opponent discards, Mars provides additional disruption. Core control tools.

Training Area

Stadium that accelerates evolution by placing damage counters or enabling faster evolution mechanics. Critical for tempo.

Professor's Research

Primary draw engine. Maximizes consistency for finding evolution pieces and supporters.

Sabrina

Gust effect to control opponent's active Pokémon. Enables KO setups and disrupts opponent positioning.

Game Plan Stages

Open

Chingling

Prioritize getting Swablu and Pumpkaboo into play early. Use Professor's Research aggressively to dig for evolution pieces and Training Area. Chingling should be benched for support utility. Poké Ball helps find missing basics. Avoid overextending—focus on establishing one evolution line (preferably Gourgeist first for early disruption). Use Copycat or Lisia for additional draw if Research isn't available. Training Area should be played as soon as possible to accelerate evolutions. If facing aggro, prioritize Gourgeist setup for healing/stall; against slower decks, rush Mega Altaria ex.

Mid

Gourgeist

Transition into disruption mode. Use Cyrus and Mars to strip opponent's hand resources while Gourgeist applies pressure and heals. Mega Altaria ex should be evolving or preparing to attack. Training Area continues to provide value if still in play. Sabrina becomes critical for pulling up vulnerable targets or disrupting opponent's setup. Manage energy attachments carefully—prioritize the active attacker (Gourgeist early, Mega Altaria ex later). Use Jirachi (if included) for additional utility. Avoid trading unfavorably; the deck wins through attrition, not speed. Rocky Helmet (if included) punishes contact damage.

Close

Mega Altaria ex

Mega Altaria ex takes over as the primary attacker, leveraging its bulk to outlast opponents. Continue hand disruption with Cyrus/Mars to prevent opponent comebacks. Gourgeist can pivot back in for healing if Mega Altaria ex needs recovery time. Sabrina secures final KOs by pulling up damaged or low-HP targets. Manage prize trades carefully—avoid giving up easy KOs on basics. The deck's win condition is grinding opponents out of resources and options, then finishing with Mega Altaria ex's consistent damage. Copycat provides late-game draw to maintain hand size and options.

  • Jirachi (0.67 avg count, 67% presence): Utility Pokémon providing additional effects (likely search or draw). Flexible tech for consistency.
  • Rocky Helmet (0.33 avg count, 33% presence): Punishes opponent's attacks with recoil damage. Strong against aggressive decks that attack frequently.
  • Training Area (1.0 avg count, 100% presence): Core stadium for accelerating evolutions. Essential for tempo and not truly optional.
  • Cyrus + Mars (1.0 each, 100% presence): Hand disruption package. Cyrus is the primary disruptor, Mars provides additional pressure. Both are core to the control strategy.
