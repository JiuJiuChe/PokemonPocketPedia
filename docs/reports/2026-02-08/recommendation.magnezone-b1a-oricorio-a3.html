Game Plan

Magnezone Oricorio is a Stage 2 evolution deck that aims to establish Magnezone as a high-HP, high-damage attacker while using Oricorio as a versatile early-game pivot and energy accelerator. The deck leverages Clemont and Cyrus for control, Giant Cape for durability, and Pokémon Center Lady for sustain. Win by evolving Magnezone quickly, controlling the opponent's board state with disruption supporters, and applying consistent pressure with Magnezone's attacks while Oricorio provides early tempo and energy support.

Key Cards and Roles

Magnezone

Primary attacker with high HP and damage output. Stage 2 evolution requiring Magnemite → Magneton → Magnezone.

Oricorio

Early-game attacker and energy accelerator. Provides tempo and supports Magnezone setup.

Magnemite

Evolution line for Magnezone. Prioritize benching Magnemite early.

Cyrus

Disruption supporter that limits opponent's hand size and board control.

Clemont

Energy acceleration for Electric Pokémon, critical for powering up Magnezone.

Giant Cape

Increases Magnezone's HP, making it harder to KO.

Pokémon Center Lady

Heals damage, extending Magnezone's longevity.

Professor's Research

Draw engines for consistency and hand refresh.

Game Plan Stages

Open

Magnemite

Mulligan for Magnemite or Oricorio. Ideal opening hand includes Magnemite, Oricorio, Poké Ball, and Professor's Research. Turn 1: Bench Magnemite immediately using Poké Ball or manual draw. If possible, start with Oricorio active to apply early pressure and accelerate energy. Use Professor's Research to dig for evolution pieces (Magneton) and supporters. Avoid overcommitting energy to Oricorio if Magnezone pieces are in hand—prioritize setting up the bench. Turn 2-3: Continue searching for Magneton and Magnezone. Use Copycat or Lisia (if included) to maintain hand size and find key cards. Attach energy to Magnemite/Magneton or Oricorio depending on board state.

Mid

Magneton

Turn 4-6: Evolve into Magnezone as soon as possible. Use Clemont to accelerate energy onto Magnezone, enabling attacks quickly. Oricorio should transition to a support role or sacrifice piece if needed. Apply Cyrus to disrupt opponent's hand and slow their setup. Attach Giant Cape to Magnezone to increase survivability. Use Pokémon Center Lady reactively to heal Magnezone after taking damage, preserving your main attacker. Manage energy attachments carefully—prioritize Magnezone but keep Oricorio energized if it's still active. Use Copycat to maintain hand resources and avoid dead turns. Control the pace by forcing opponent to deal with a high-HP Magnezone while disrupting their resources.

Close

Magnezone

Turn 7+: Magnezone should be fully powered and attacking consistently. Use Giant Cape and Pokémon Center Lady to keep Magnezone alive through multiple exchanges. Apply Cyrus repeatedly to limit opponent's options and prevent comebacks. If Magnezone is KO'd, pivot to a second Magnezone if available, or use Oricorio as a cleanup attacker. Lisia (if included) can search for critical tools or supporters to close out the game. Red Card (if included) can disrupt opponent's hand at critical moments. Focus on taking KOs efficiently—Magnezone's high damage should secure knockouts on key threats. Avoid overextending; preserve resources for final turns if the game goes long.

Tech Choices

  • Cyrus (1-2 copies): Core disruption tool to limit opponent's hand and board control. Essential against combo and control decks.
  • Clemont (1 copy): Energy acceleration for Electric types. Critical for powering up Magnezone quickly.
  • Giant Cape (1 copy): Increases Magnezone's HP, making it more resilient against high-damage threats.
  • Pokémon Center Lady (1 copy): Healing support to extend Magnezone's lifespan. Strong in grindy matchups.
  • Lisia (0-1 copies): Flexible search card for supporters or tools. Optional but increases consistency.
  • Guzma (0-1 copies): Forces opponent's benched Pokémon active. Useful for targeting weak bench sitters or disrupting setup.
  • Red Card (0-1 copies): Hand disruption to disrupt opponent's strategy at key moments.
  • Lucky Ice Pop (0-1 copies): Additional healing option. Competes with Pokémon Center Lady for deck space.
  • Mars (0-1 copies): Alternative disruption supporter. Less common but can replace Cyrus in some builds.
