Game Plan

Mega Kangaskhan ex Darkrai ex is a disruptive control deck that leverages baby Pokémon (Pichu, Mantyke, Magby, Tyrogue) to stall early game while building toward Mega Kangaskhan ex as the primary win condition. Darkrai ex provides secondary pressure and type coverage. The deck uses hand disruption (Ilima, Cyrus) and positioning tools (Sabrina, Serena) to control tempo, aiming to outlast opponents through resource denial and efficient trades. With a 46.5% win rate and 2.8% meta share, this is a niche strategy that rewards precise sequencing and matchup knowledge.

Key Cards and Roles

Mega Kangaskhan ex

Primary attacker and win condition. High HP and damage output make it the deck's late-game closer.

Darkrai ex

Secondary attacker providing Dark-type coverage and pressure against Psychic-weak targets.

Pichu

Early-game stall tools with coin-flip immunity effects, buying time to set up Mega Kangaskhan ex.

Ilima

Hand disruption supporter forcing opponent to shuffle and redraw, disrupting their setup.

Cyrus

Targeted hand disruption removing key cards from opponent's hand.

Giovanni

Damage modifier enabling key KO thresholds on Mega Kangaskhan ex or Darkrai ex attacks.

Red

Emergency comeback tool providing card draw and board reset potential.

Professor's Research

Primary draw engine for deck consistency.

Game Plan Stages

Open

Mega Kangaskhan ex

Prioritize benching baby Pokémon (Pichu, Mantyke, Magby, or Tyrogue) as your active to absorb early damage with coin-flip immunity. Use Professor's Research aggressively to dig for Mega Kangaskhan ex and energy attachments. Avoid committing Darkrai ex or Mega Kangaskhan ex to the active spot early unless necessary. Use Poké Ball (0.67 avg presence) to search for missing pieces. Focus on energy attachment to your primary attacker on the bench while the baby Pokémon stalls. Deploy Ilima early if opponent shows strong setup potential to disrupt their hand.

Mid

Professor's Research

Transition from baby Pokémon to Mega Kangaskhan ex once it has sufficient energy to threaten KOs. Use Cyrus or Ilima to disrupt opponent's hand before they execute key plays. Giovanni should be saved for securing critical KO thresholds on high-value targets. Deploy Sabrina or Serena to pull damaged benched Pokémon forward or reposition threats. Darkrai ex can be brought in as a secondary attacker if Mega Kangaskhan ex is threatened or if Dark-type coverage is needed. Manage energy attachments carefully between both attackers to maintain pressure. Use Red if you fall behind on board state or need emergency draw.

Close

Giovanni

Mega Kangaskhan ex should be your primary closer, leveraging its high HP and damage output to take consecutive KOs. Use Giovanni to push damage into lethal range on key targets. Sabrina or Serena can force out damaged benched Pokémon for easy prizes. If Mega Kangaskhan ex is knocked out, pivot to Darkrai ex as a backup attacker. Maintain hand disruption pressure with Ilima or Cyrus to prevent opponent comebacks. Prioritize taking prizes efficiently rather than overextending resources. Red can be used as a last-resort comeback tool if you're behind on prizes.
