Game Plan

This deck is a dual-line setup deck centered on Mega Altaria ex plus the Pumpkaboo -> Gourgeist line, supported by high-consistency draw/search/disruption. The observed structure is highly fixed in sampled lists: core attackers/evolutions are mostly 2-of with 100% presence_rate (Mega Altaria ex, Swablu, Pumpkaboo, Gourgeist, Chingling, Professor's Research), while supporters/utility are mostly 1-of (Copycat, Cyrus, Lisia, Mars, Sabrina, Poké Ball, Training Area, Jirachi). In ranked play, the practical plan is to establish one primary attacking line quickly, preserve bench depth for the second line, and use singleton supporters for tempo swings.

Game Plan Stages

Open

Chingling

Mulligan decisions should prioritize opening with at least one base from either evolution line (Swablu or Pumpkaboo) and a consistency enabler (Professor's Research and/or Poké Ball). In turn sequencing, prefer establishing both lines over overcommitting to one attacker too early, because your deck construction shows two complete 2-2 style cores with full presence_rate support. Use Professor's Research early when it materially improves evolution assembly; hold singleton disruption supporters if no immediate tempo gain is available.

Mid

Gourgeist

By midgame, convert your board into at least one active evolved threat while preparing the second line on bench as insurance. Use the singleton supporter suite (Copycat/Cyrus/Lisia/Mars/Sabrina) as tactical responses rather than automatic plays, since each is only avg_count 1.0 and therefore high-opportunity-cost. Against top-field decks, maintain pressure through continuous attacker handoff: if one line is disrupted, transition quickly to the other line to avoid tempo loss.

Close

Mega Altaria ex

In closing turns, commit resources to the attacker line that gives the shortest and safest finish path, and avoid splitting your final support cards across low-impact turns. Preserve at least one tempo/disruption supporter for the deciding sequence when possible, since singleton supporter timing is your main edge in close games. If your primary threat is checked, pivot immediately to the alternate evolved line rather than trying to rebuild from basics late.

Common Pitfalls

  • Overcommitting early to only one evolution line and losing tempo when that line is disrupted.
  • Spending singleton supporters on marginal turns; these 1-of cards are highest value when timed for swing turns.
  • Using Professor's Research without planning around remaining evolution pieces, causing awkward midgame rebuilds.
  • Cutting too many core 2-of pieces during tuning; sample data shows the archetype’s stability comes from fixed core counts.
  • Assuming all substitute candidates are equally valid; many listed Pokémon swaps have non-specific compatibility metadata (category=None), so role fit is uncertain.
