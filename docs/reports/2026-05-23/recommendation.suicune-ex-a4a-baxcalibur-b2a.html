Game Plan

Suicune ex Baxcalibur is the #1 meta deck (15.92% share, 794 samples) with 50.54% win rate. Core strategy uses dual-ex Pokemon structure: Frigibax→Baxcalibur provides early board presence with 2 avg_count per sample, while Suicune ex serves as the late-game finisher with Irida healing support. Rare Candy accelerates evolution to Stage 2. Giant Cape provides +20 HP durability. Copycat and Mars offer resource cycling and disruption. The deck excels at establishing multiple Pokemon and maintaining health through Irida's healing effect on Water-energy Pokemon.

Game Plan Stages

Open

Baxcalibur

Keep hands with Frigibax, Rare Candy, and draw cards (Professor's Research, Irida, Poké Ball). Look for Frigibax x2 to establish early board presence. Use Poké Ball to search for additional Frigibax or Baxcalibur. If Rare Candy is drawn early and Frigibax is in play, consider accelerating to Baxcalibur. Irida should be retained for mid-turn healing. Starting Plains provides +20 HP buff to basic Pokemon if played early. Sample data shows 2 Frigibax avg_count indicates dual-baseline for reliable setup.

Mid

Frigibax

Execute Frigibax→Baxcalibur evolution using Rare Candy for accelerated threat. Establish Suicune ex on bench for alternate finisher. Use Irida each turn to heal Water-energy Pokemon, maintaining board presence. Attach Giant Cape to primary attacker or Suicune ex for durability. Copycat can repeat Professor's Research for sustained card flow. Mars disrupts opponent's hand when they have 2+ remaining turns to win. Starting Plains +20 HP buff on both players' basics adds defensive buffer. If Baxcalibur takes damage, use Irida to maintain health pool.

Close

Poké Ball

When Suicune ex is ready, attack with high-damage option to finish game. Maintain at least one ex on board as finisher. Use Irida to heal any ex Pokemon before opponent can capitalize on weakened state. If opponent has high remaining health, consider using Suicune ex's ability for massive damage or Chien-Pao ex as alternate finisher. Preserve Mars/Cyrus for disruption if opponent attempts late-game comeback. With Giant Cape on board, ex Pokemon gains +20 HP, increasing survival window. Ensure at least one healer is active before committing to final attack.
