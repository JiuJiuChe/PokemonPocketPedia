Game Plan

This is a dual-evolution setup deck that leans on Greninja as the primary repeated pressure core while using a lighter Decidueye ex package as secondary threat utility. The identity is strongly consistency-driven: Copycat, Greninja, Poké Ball, Professor's Research, and Rare Candy are all avg_count 2.0 with presence_rate 1.0, indicating the deck wins most when it assembles evolution lines quickly and keeps hand flow high. In ranked play, the plan is to establish Greninja line first, then deploy Decidueye ex line opportunistically, while using mobility/tempo cards (X Speed, Hiking Trail) to keep attacks and pivots efficient.

Game Plan Stages

Open

Copycat

Prioritize opening with at least one starter from your Greninja line and a consistency card (Poké Ball or Professor's Research). Use early turns to secure evolution progression first; avoid spending flexible utility cards unless they directly convert into faster setup or immediate board safety. If both lines are available, commit resources to the Greninja route first because its core counts are denser and more stable in samples than the Decidueye ex package.

Mid

Greninja

Transition into sustained pressure once Greninja is online, then layer Decidueye ex when the board state rewards a second threat line. Sequence Copycat/Research to avoid hand depletion and preserve evolution continuity. Use Hiking Trail and X Speed for tempo turns where positioning changes directly convert into damage or deny opponent stabilization. Because this archetype underperforms currently (win_rate_pct 47.74), prioritize low-variance lines and guaranteed attacks over speculative tech plays.

Close

Poké Ball

In endgame, commit to the attacker line that provides the shortest guaranteed finish and conserve one interaction/positioning card for the decisive turn. If the secondary line is incomplete, do not overforce it; continue with the established Greninja pressure plan. Protect attack continuity and board access over greedier value sequencing, since missed attacks are especially costly in a sub-50% deck shell.

Tech Choices

  • Keep the proven core fixed first (Copycat, Greninja, Poké Ball, Professor's Research, Rare Candy all avg_count 2.0 and presence_rate 1.0).
  • Primary tuning should happen in supporter and minor item slots before cutting evolution core cards.
  • Use trainer_type-matched substitutions when possible (Supporter-to-Supporter, Item-to-Item) because they preserve structure better than broad Pokemon swaps.
  • Given slight positive share trend (+0.66 1d/7d) but weak win rate (47.74), tune for consistency and matchup stability rather than full archetype pivots.

Common Pitfalls

  • Overcommitting resources to the lighter Decidueye ex line before securing Greninja consistency.
  • Using Rare Candy or search effects without planning the full next-turn evolution sequence.
  • Spending mobility/tempo cards (X Speed/Hiking Trail) on low-impact turns instead of conversion turns.
  • Cutting core 2-of consistency cards while teching, which amplifies variance in an already low-win-rate deck.
  • Treating category=None or broad Pokemon swap candidates as strict upgrades; many imply archetype drift rather than side-grades.
