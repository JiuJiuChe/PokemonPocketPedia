## Core Pocket-oriented Differences (Working Summary)
- Deck size is 20 cards.
- Copies per card name are typically capped at 2.
- Pocket uses an Energy Zone model instead of including Energy cards in the deck.
- Point-based win condition is used (instead of standard prize-card flow in mainline TCG).
- Bench size is smaller than mainline TCG.
- Resistance handling differs; practical Pocket play references often treat resistance as absent.
- First-turn behavior differs from mainline in draw/supporter/attachment constraints.
- Deck-out loss handling differs from mainline in practical Pocket guidance.

## Strategy Implications
- Consistency is naturally higher due to no Energy card draws from deck.
- Low deck size increases value of card quality, redundancy planning, and sequencing.
- 2-copy cap increases pressure on role coverage and substitute planning.
- Point race matters more than long prize mapping; tempo and KO thresholds are central.
- Bench-slot competition is tighter; prioritize board slots by win condition.

## How To Apply In Analysis
- Treat Energy acceleration commentary in terms of zone access/tempo, not draw odds of Energy cards.
- Evaluate deck cards by role compression because of 20-card limit and 2-copy cap.
- Frame matchup advice around point race breakpoints and bench pressure.
